## Why

Traders need to see at a glance where each ticker sits within its options-implied expected move range. A ticker near the top of its weekly EM is "extended" (risky for new longs), while one near the bottom is at potential support. Currently this data exists in `data/expected-moves.json` but has no visual representation.

## ⚠️ Core EM Rules — READ THIS

### What "Close" Means (Anchor Price)
- **Close = the price at which the EM range was calculated.** It is the yfinance `lastPrice` at update time.
- The EM range is symmetrical around this close: `lower = close - EM`, `upper = close + EM`.
- The close is NOT "Friday close" — it is whatever price yfinance returns at script run time.
- **This is the anchor price.** If it's stale, the entire range is wrong.

### When EM Ranges Must Be Refreshed
- **EOD pipeline** runs Mon-Fri at 4:20 PM ET via GitHub Actions (`eod-update.sh`).
  - Friday: all tiers (weekly + monthly + quarterly straddles).
  - Mon-Thu: daily tier only (8 index proxies), weekly/monthly/quarterly preserved from Friday.
- **Manual runs:** `python3 scripts/update-expected-moves.py --source yfinance` (any time).
- **⚠️ CRITICAL:** Running during market hours uses the LIVE intraday price as "close", NOT the previous day's actual close. This creates a moving anchor — acceptable for intraday use but misleading if the page says "Updated Mar 27" and the close is an intraday snapshot, not a settled EOD value.

### How the Dashboard Displays Data (Two Sources)
1. **EM ranges** come from `data/expected-moves.json` (anchor close, upper, lower).
2. **Current price** comes from `data/prices.json` via `bt-prices.js` (live or recent).
3. **Position in range** = `(currentPrice - lower) / (upper - lower) × 100`.
4. If the anchor close is stale (e.g., 2 days old) but prices moved significantly, positions will be distorted — all tickers may appear ABOVE or BELOW EM even when the market move is within normal range.

### The Staleness Trap (Mar 27 Bug)
- EM was anchored to Mar 25 closes (SPY $657, META $598).
- Market dropped hard Mar 26-27 (SPY $633, META $525).
- Dashboard showed 100% of tickers as "ABOVE EM" in red — nonsensical.
- **Root cause:** EM ranges were stale. The fix is always: re-run `update-expected-moves.py` so the anchor close matches reality.
- **Rule:** If EM data is >24h old during a volatile market, the page is unreliable. The staleness warning (orange/red timestamp) exists but the data itself misleads.

### Scaling Rules
- `Weekly EM = ATM weekly straddle × 0.85`
- `Daily EM = Weekly EM / √5` (scaled down)
- `Monthly EM = monthly straddle × 0.85` (direct, if available) or `Weekly EM × √(21/5)` (scaled up)
- `Quarterly EM = quarterly straddle × 0.85` (direct, if available) or `Weekly EM × √(63/5)` (scaled up)
- Scaling uses `√(target_dte / source_dte)` — square root of time.

### Data Priority
- IB Gateway (live options data, best accuracy) → yfinance (free, delayed, good enough)
- IB paper account has incomplete option chains — many tickers fail. Use live account for production data.
- yfinance options: uses `lastPrice` for straddle calc. During market hours this is live; after hours it's the last traded price.

### History
- Each ticker stores up to 52 history entries (weekly snapshots) with date, close, straddle, weekly_em, daily_em.
- History dedupes by date — re-running on the same day overwrites that day's entry.

## Risk Model

Position in range = `(currentPrice - lower) / (upper - lower) × 100`

| Position | Risk Level | Color | Long Signal |
|----------|-----------|-------|-------------|
| <0% | BELOW EM | Bright green | Potential bounce — price broke below expected range |
| ≤10% | AT SUPPORT | Dark green | Strong buy zone — near bottom of expected range |
| 10-20% | NEAR LOW | Green | Buy zone |
| 20-40% | LOW | Light green | Favorable for longs |
| 40-55% | MODERATE | Yellow-green | Neutral — middle of expected range |
| 55-70% | ELEVATED | Yellow | Caution — approaching upper half |
| 70-85% | HIGH | Orange | Risky for new longs |
| 85-100% | EXTENDED | Red | Very risky — at/near EM ceiling |
| >100% | ABOVE EM | Dark red | Overextended — price broke above expected range |

### Interpreting Risk Levels
- **For LONGS:** Lower position = better entry (more room to run within the range). Higher = more extended (less room, more risk).
- **BELOW EM (< 0%)** means price dropped BELOW the expected low — options market didn't price in this much downside. Could signal capitulation/bounce, but also could mean EM data is stale.
- **ABOVE EM (> 100%)** means price exceeded the expected high — market moved more than options implied. Could signal momentum continuation or mean-reversion risk.
- **These are not buy/sell signals.** They are risk context — "how stretched is price relative to what the options market expected?"
