## 🔴 Major Crashes & Bear Markets

### 2000-2002 — Dot-Com Bust
- **Trigger:** Extreme valuations, retail mania, breadth divergence (few mega-caps leading)
- **Warning signs:** RSP/SPY divergence (equal-weight lagging = narrow leadership), margin debt ATH, IPO frenzy
- **VIX:** Spiked from ~20 to 45+
- **Drawdown:** SPX -49% (1,553 → 776), NASDAQ -78%
- **Duration:** 30 months (Mar 2000 — Oct 2002)
- **Recovery:** 7 years to reclaim highs
- **Key lesson:** "When only 5 stocks are carrying the index, the index is lying"
- **Pair signals:** RSP/SPY broke down hard before SPX topped; XLY/XLP rolled over

### 2007-2009 — Global Financial Crisis
- **Trigger:** Housing bubble, subprime, credit market freeze
- **Warning signs:** HYG/SPY broke down (credit leading equities lower), yield curve inverted 2006, homebuilders (XHB) topped in 2005 — 2 years early
- **VIX:** 80+ (highest ever at the time)
- **Drawdown:** SPX -57% (1,576 → 666)
- **Duration:** 17 months (Oct 2007 — Mar 2009)
- **Key levels:** SPX 666 = generational bottom. Put/Call ratio >1.2 at bottom = extreme fear
- **Key lesson:** "If bonds freak out, everything breaks" — credit markets led equities by months
- **Pair signals:** HYG/SPY topped in Jun 2007 (4 months before SPX). XLY/XLP broke down.
- **Tom's take:** "The bond market is always right. When credit spreads blow out, run."

### 2011 — Euro Debt Crisis / US Downgrade
- **Trigger:** S&P downgraded US debt from AAA, Greek debt crisis
- **Drawdown:** SPX -19.4% (Jul-Oct 2011)
- **VIX:** Spiked to 48
- **Recovery:** 5 months
- **Key lesson:** Corrections in bull markets are buying opportunities when credit stays stable
- **Pair signals:** HYG/SPY held relatively well = not a systemic credit event

### 2015-2016 — China Deval / Oil Crash
- **Trigger:** China devalued yuan, oil crashed below $30, manufacturing recession fears
- **Drawdown:** SPX -14% (Aug 2015), another -13% (Jan-Feb 2016)
- **VIX:** 40+ in Aug 2015
- **Recovery:** Bottomed Feb 2016 at 1,810, V-recovery
- **Key lesson:** "Commodity crashes cause fear but don't always lead to recession"
- **Key level:** SPX 200 SMA held both times = bull market intact

### 2018 Q4 — Fed Tightening Tantrum
- **Trigger:** Fed hiking into slowing economy + trade war + quantitative tightening
- **Warning signs:** Yield curve flattening, small caps (IWM) diverging from large caps, VIX from 12 → 36
- **Drawdown:** SPX -19.8% (Sep-Dec 2018)
- **Bottom:** Dec 24, 2018 ("Christmas Eve Massacre") — SPX 2,346
- **Recovery:** Fed pivoted to cuts → V-recovery in Q1 2019
- **Key lesson:** "Fed hike cycles always break something" — Tom's rule
- **Pair signals:** IWF/IWD (growth vs value) broke down, RSP/SPY diverged
- **Sentiment at bottom:** AAII bears >50%, NAAIM near lows = textbook contrarian buy

### 2020 — COVID Crash
- **Trigger:** Global pandemic, lockdowns
- **Speed:** Fastest 30% decline in history (23 trading days)
- **Drawdown:** SPX -34% (Feb 19 — Mar 23, 2020), 3,393 → 2,237
- **VIX:** 82.69 (all-time high)
- **Recovery:** V-shaped. New ATH by Aug 2020 (5 months)
- **Key lesson:** Sentiment extremes + massive fiscal/monetary response = buy signal
- **Put/Call:** Equity P/C >0.93 with SPX >10% off highs → 100% of the time higher 12mo, median +22%
- **Dark pool flows:** Massive institutional buying at the bottom, retail was panic selling
- **Tom's take:** "Follow the flows, not the fear. Smart money was buying when CNN Fear & Greed hit 2."

### 2022 — Rate Hike Bear Market
- **Trigger:** Inflation 9.1%, Fed hiked from 0% → 5.25% fastest pace since 1980s
- **Warning signs:** Yield curve inversion (2s10s), growth stocks already falling from Nov 2021, ARKK -75%
- **Drawdown:** SPX -25.4% (Jan-Oct 2022), NASDAQ -33%
- **Bottom:** Oct 12, 2022 — SPX 3,491
- **Duration:** 10 months
- **Key lesson:** "When the Fed is hiking, don't fight it. But when they signal a pause, start buying."
- **Pair signals:** IWF/IWD collapsed (growth destroyed), XLV/SPY outperformed (defensive rotation)
- **Sentiment at bottom:** AAII bears >60%, NAAIM at 20 = extreme pessimism = contrarian buy

## 📊 Pattern Templates (for matching against current conditions)

### Pattern: "Narrow Leadership Divergence"
- **Historical:** 1999-2000, 2021 (FAANG carrying index)
- **Signal:** RSP/SPY declining while SPX makes new highs
- **Implication:** Fragile rally, vulnerable to rotation or de-risking
- **Tom's warning threshold:** RSP/SPY 3-month trend down while SPX up = amber flag

### Pattern: "Credit Leads Equities"
- **Historical:** 2007 (HYG topped 4mo before SPX), 2020 (HYG bottomed with SPX)
- **Signal:** HYG/SPY breaking down while SPX holds
- **Implication:** "If bonds freak out, everything breaks"
- **Tom's rule:** HYG/SPY weekly EMA 13 cross below 26 = reduce risk immediately

### Pattern: "Fed Pivot Trade"
- **Historical:** 2019 (cuts after 2018 hikes), 2020 (unlimited QE), 2023 (pause → cut signal)
- **Signal:** Fed language shifts from hawkish to neutral/dovish
- **Implication:** Major buy signal, especially with bearish sentiment
- **Tom's rule:** "Fed hike cycles always break something. The pivot is the buy."

### Pattern: "Retail vs Smart Money Divergence"
- **Historical:** Every major top and bottom
- **Signal:** Dumb Money Confidence >0.7 while Smart Money <0.3 (or vice versa)
- **Implication:** >0.7 dumb + <0.3 smart = distribution top forming. Reverse = accumulation bottom.
- **Source:** SentimenTrader

### Pattern: "VIX Complacency Trap"
- **Historical:** Jan 2018 (VIX 9 → 50), Feb 2020 (VIX 14 → 82)
- **Signal:** VIX <15 for extended period while uncertainties build
- **Implication:** "When VIX is cheap, everyone is complacent. That's when it explodes."
- **Tom's rule:** VIX <15 + geopolitical risk rising = buy VIX hedges

### Pattern: "Put/Call Capitulation Buy"
- **Historical:** 2009, 2011, 2018, 2020, 2022
- **Signal:** Equity Put/Call >0.93 with SPX >10% off highs
- **Implication:** 100% of the time higher 12 months later, median return +22%
- **Tom's most reliable rule**

### Pattern: "Yield Curve Un-Inversion Warning"
- **Historical:** Before every recession since 1970s
- **Signal:** 2s10s yield curve inverts, then UN-inverts (steepens)
- **Implication:** Recession typically starts 6-18 months AFTER un-inversion
- **Key distinction:** Inversion = warning. Un-inversion = countdown started.
- **Tom's note:** ">50% of curve inverted = recession risk elevated"

### Pattern: "20 DMA Break = Correction Risk"
- **Historical:** Tom's bread and butter — tested across decades
- **Signal:** SPX closes below 20 DMA with increasing volume
- **Implication:** 7-9% correction risk within 2-4 weeks
- **Tom's rule:** "20 MA is king. Break it on volume and you're looking at 50 MA test minimum."

### Pattern: "Money Market Surge = Pre-Crisis"
- **Historical:** 2007-2008, 2019-2020
- **Signal:** Money market fund assets spike to new records
- **Implication:** Smart money parking cash = expecting trouble. BUT also = dry powder for eventual recovery.
- **Dual signal:** Bearish in the moment (risk-off), but bullish for eventual recovery (fuel)

### Pattern: "Margin Debt ATH = Top Warning"
- **Historical:** 2000, 2007, 2021
- **Signal:** NYSE margin debt at all-time highs while market extended
- **Implication:** Excess leverage = forced selling cascade when correction starts
- **Tom's take:** "Retail piling in with leverage = be suspicious"

## 📈 Sector Rotation Sequences (Tom's Framework)

### Late Cycle → Recession Rotation
1. Technology peaks first (growth premium evaporates)
2. Consumer Discretionary (XLY) weakens → XLY/XLP ratio breaks down
3. Financials weaken (yield curve, credit tightening)
4. Utilities (XLU), Healthcare (XLV), Staples (XLP) outperform — defensive rotation
5. Energy sometimes last to peak (oil spike on geopolitical = final gasp)

### Recession → Recovery Rotation
1. Small caps (IWM) lead — most sensitive to credit conditions improving
2. Financials early (yield curve steepening)
3. Consumer Discretionary recovers (XLY/XLP turns up)
4. Technology re-accelerates mid-cycle
5. Defensive sectors lag (money flows out of safe havens)
